Lesh go!

[01. Object]

Facilitate and encourage the pursuit of endurance and adventure running through organised and hosted events for the benefit of members.

[02. Context]

Classic and grassroots wilderness races are disappearing due to administrative and compliance costs. The events have never been about commercial gain. Often without sponsors, no loot bags, big banners and affiliate marketing. There weren’t the waitlists, or festival line-ups of the modern day event.

Establishing an organised group provides for certain and specific exemption to regulations, concessions, and health and safety legislation.

The expensive and bureaucratic compliance cost and administrative burden would be avoided by establishing an incorporated society. Races, events or activities would be non-commercial recreational activity for its members.

Conservation Act 1987 includes an exemption (s17O(4)) from needing a DOC concession for certain activities on public conservation land

A club, society, or group can run activities for its own members on conservation land without a concession, as long as the activity is non-commercial.

The activity must not be promoted or operated as a commercial event, and not be open to the general public in the way a paid race would be.

[03. Membership]

Participation requires formal membership in the Incorporated Society.

  • - Events are strictly member-only.
  • - No public entries or external marketing.
  • - Event fees are purely for cost-recovery (i.e the sausage).

A “Subscription Model:” members would pay an annual sub. to cover all other society expenses.

The sub. would be calculated on the greatest value of participating in just a single event + inclusions

Society members could be members of FMC (the Federated Mountain Clubs), the organisation could reserve capital for rescue, or donate to other like-minded community groups.

[04. Health and Safety]

Ensuring safe adventure does not mean hiding behind legalistic paperwork.

  • - Most community groups don’t have duties under the HSW Act
  • - The society would not have employees to avoid WorkSafe obligations.
  • - We have ACC.
  • - Members would be responsible for themselves.
  • - We can have our own policies (or bylaws) to ensure we keep our members safe.

A “volunteer association” (defined as a group without employees) is largely exempt from the HSW Act’s main duties.

A volunteer association is not considered a PCBU (Person Conducting a Business or Undertaking).

Activities carried out by a sports club or association for its members, where the activity is non-commercial are excluded from The HSW (Adventure Activities) Regulations 2016.

[07. FAQ]

Is this legal?
Yes. This follows the established model of tramping and recreation clubs. Just the type of recreation is leaning into Type II and III fun.

Events can still be timed; the results would be of our the members. It can still be recreation even with a stop-watch.

We can encourage membership, through the event line-up (Mukamuka Munter and Aorangi Undulator, perhaps Wellington Trampers' Marathon, and any "races" the members wish to partake or see established.

An example?
See Fearsociety.co.nz or Hüttcross Inc. which subscribes members on race entry.

Who manages the events?
The society must remain volunteer-led to avoid scutiny, and keep costs accessible to all members.

The events can be run by the usual people, however they needn't deal with mountains of paperwork.

What makes it different from Big Sunday Runs (BSR)?
Its formal, but maybe not as regulated as an Athlettuce NZ club. BSR runners might be members of W.R.E.C’kd, but not all W.R.E.C’kd members would identify BSR. You could still hold membership with Trentham United, Thorndon Bridge Club and the Wellington Canoe Polo Association

How many members?
Probably 160 odd, depending on interest for the events. (Based on 2023 Mukamuka Entries)
